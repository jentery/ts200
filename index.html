MODULE 1: Memory, Novelty, Obsolescence

Questions

KEYWORDS: affect (Anna Munster), angel of history (Walter Benjamin), archaeology (Wolfgang Ernst and Jussi Parikka), entanglement (Karen Barad), formats (Jonathan Sterne), index (Mary Ann Doane), infrastructure (Shannon Mattern), interface (Lori Emerson), magic (Carolyn Marvin), memory (Wendy Hui Kyong Chun), new media (Lisa Gitelman and Lev Manovich), planned obsolescence (Giles Slade and Kathleen Fitzpatrick), remediation (Jay David Bolter and Richard Grusin), and storage (Matthew Kirschenbaum)

PROTOTYPING TECHNIQUE: 2-D to 3-D translation

M 11 Sept: Watch (before class) "First Sounds: Humanity's First Recordings of Its Own Voice" (David Giovannoni) and "When Games Went Click: The Story of Tennis for Two" (Raiford Guins, Laine Nooney, Vlad Nudin, et al.) | Form Groups (in class) | Lecture: From Inventors and Innovation to Mess and Maintenance | Workshop: Logging Your Work in this Course

R 14 Sept: Read (before class) "Society" (Glenn Hendler) and "Technology" (me) | Lecture: Beyond Tools and Instruments | Workshop: Finding and Reading Patents

M 18 Sept: Read "Funes the Memorious" (Jorge Luis Borges) and "The Story of Cold Dark Matter" (Cornelia Parker and Tate) | Lecture: Re-membering the Past | Workshop: The Exploded View

R 21 Sept: Research and Develop Your First Prototype (before and during class) | Workshop: Absence, Form, and Practice in Patents

M 25 Sept: Finish and Present Your First Prototype (before and during class)

LOG (11 Sept - 25 Sept): Find a patent (dated between 1850 and 1950) for a technology that no longer exists, is no longer popular, or both. Use materials of your choice (e.g., paper, cardboard, plasticine, or software) to bring your technology off the page, translating the 2-D patent into a 3-D form. Bonus points if you can get your prototype to work. Log your research and prototyping as you progress through the module, including documentation of the prototyping process as well as notes from the workshops, lectures, and assigned readings and video.
